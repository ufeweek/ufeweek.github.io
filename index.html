ABOUT

Engineers’ Week at the University of Florida is a two-week celebration, hosted by the Benton Engineering Council through UF’s College of Engineering, that aims to recognize engineers’ contribution to modern social development through a series of fun, interactive, and informative events. While Engineers’ Week aims to celebrate engineers, the events also underscore the relationship between engineering and all other fields of study, from fine arts and journalism to liberal arts and social sciences.

E-Talk

February 13, 2017
6:00-8:00 PM
J. Wayne Reitz Union Auditorium

Engineers’ Talk is an event which seeks to answer one question: “What does engineering look like in the modern world?” We invite engineers from both industry and academia to discuss their experiences, their ideas, and their stories from working in the field of engineering. With a focus on innovation, collaboration, and global impact, the presentations of this year’s E-Talk will help us all Explore New Horizons.

Bowling with Faculty

February 16, 2017
6:00-8:00 PM
J. Wayne Reitz Union Game Room

Bowling with Faculty provides a unique opportunity for all students, professors, and advisors in the College of Engineering. Students get the chance to know some of the most influential people in the field of engineering and broaden their networks in a night of free fun and games, while faculty members can get to know their students and share their experiences in a more casual setting than a classroom or office. Take this opportunity to expand your network and explore new horizons all while enjoying free bowling!

E-Fest

February 18, 2017
12:00-5:00 PM
Flavet Field

Relax and let loose in a nonwork setting! A welcoming atmosphere for event attendees of all majors, E-Fest will be set by live local musicians and acts, carnival games/activities, and FREE food. Some activities include tie dying FREE E-Fest T-shirts, an inflatable jousting arena, a dunk tank, bubble soccer, a rock climbing wall, All-Star basketball, and life size jenga.

Built to Battle

February 19, 2017
8:00 AM-5:00 PM
New Physics Building Lobby

Team up to build a bot and battle for your chance to be the best! Built to Battle will be a beetle weight (3lb limit) battle robot competition. Components used for these bots are similar to hobby airplanes and helicopters and the tournament will be suitable for all levels of experience. New builders can gain skills as they learn the new system, while experienced builders can test their wits vs others. We want to spark the building spirit to all those in and around Gainesville, so please ask how you can get entered to compete.

Engineerng Leadership Forum

February 23, 2017
6:00-9:00 PM
The Arredondo Cafe - J. Wayne Reitz Union

Engineering Leadership Forum (ELF) promotes professional development and exposes attendees to a wide variety of opportunities within engineering and related fields. A diverse group of speakers from industry, the UF faculty, and student organizations will share their experiences and talk to attendees about the role engineering has played in their lives. Attendees will be exposed to research, academia, study abroad, and internship opporunities. Throughout the event, attendees will be able to network with attending VIPs inbetween sessions and ask questions.

Closing Ceremonies

February 24, 2017
5:30-7:30 PM
J. Wayne Reitz Union Rion Ballroom

Closing Ceremonies celebrates the success of E-Week 2017. During this event, there will be three speakers giving experiences of how engineering impacted them and what they brought to the community through engineering. This event is an invite-only event for the presidents of the societies through the engineering community here at UF.
